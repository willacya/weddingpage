Hunton Park Hotel


This magnificent beauty wasn't only chosen because it's five minutes up the road. Set within 22 acres of beautiful Hertfordshire parkland, Hunton Park is a stunning Queen Ann mansion house with traditional Georgian proportions and striking tall white columns. It's also where the Groom's sister once interviewed for Harry Potter.

Attire
  • Ladies
  • Gentlemen

If you're like the Groom (white and clueless), this section might be of help.

It is totally up to you whether you wear a suit/dress or a kurtha/saree, but it would be great to see some of you in traditonal hindu wedding attire. They're not too expensive and pretty comfortable.

For the civil ceremony the Groom and Groomsmen will change into suits, but there is nothing to stop you wearing your chosen outfit all day. Alternatively you can bring both and change at the venue.

We've put links with some suggestions of where you can buy your clothes below.

Ladies

These two stores are ones that I (Gajeni) use. They are located on Ealing road, Wembley., but do have online orders for delivery.


https://varietysilkhouse.com/

If you look for printed or embroidered sarees on the left hand menu there are a few which you might like. These saree’s are available for around £49.

A second option is Krishna Sarees which offer some beautiful sarees for a little cheaper

www.krishnasarees.com

Alternatively, we have a selection of lovely Saree’s that we’d be happy for you to borrow. Please send us a message if you’re interested and we’d be happy to show you.

How to wear a saree?

Traditionally a cropped blouse is worn underneath the saree that can be with or without sleeves. When wearing a saree you can have a modern twist by wearing a strappy crop top that matches with the sareee, instead of the traditional blouse. This might be the easiest option as not all sarees come with a ready made blouse.

Gentlemen

If you fancy yourself in a Kurta (this is the traditional Sri Lankan wedding attire), you can buy these from the same stores as the sarees.



www.krishnasarees.com

For shoes, loafers are a perfect match or you can search online for men's Mojari shoes.

Thinking of staying the night?


A few of our guests will already be staying the night at Hunton Park (including the Bride and Groom).

If you fancy staying the night before or after the wedding let us know and we can reserve one for you at a discounted rate (~£70). Traditionally I'm told Hindu weddings are strickly vegetarian and have no alcohol, but since it's a half Hindu wedding we're going to lose the meat and keep the drinks. 🥂 So staying a night might be an idea if you're planning on driving.

Social Distancing


--UPDATE--

The government has asked no more than 30 people should attend weddings and our venue is complying with this request.

This includes any staff such as caterer's, photographers, and priests. We are hoping this will change but because of this we have had to ask some of our friends not to come. It's truly the hardest decision we've had to make and hope those we can no longer invite understand. We will be quick to reverse these decisions should anything change and can only apologise for now.

Let's address the elephant in the room. With the need to social distance and being wary of any vunerable guests, we want to take every precaution available to make sure our special day is an enjoyable and safe day for all.

For social distancing we hope for most of the day to be outside but do have enough space should the weather not go our way. We'll be sure to update you if there are any changes.
